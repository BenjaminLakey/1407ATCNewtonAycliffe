What we do

Drill & Discipline

As a uniformed organisation that is part of the wider Royal Air Force. All cadets will be taught how to correctly wear uniform and how to conduct themselves on a parade. As well as teaching self discipline it gives every cadet the opportunity to be proud in being part of the UK's premier youth organisation.

Flying

We wouldn't be called the RAF Air Cadets if we didnt fly! All cadets will get the opportunity to fly in a number of powered and non powered RAF aircraft. Some lucky cadets have even flown in Chinooks and Hawks!

Adventure Training

Fancy Canoeing/Kayaking, Mountain biking, high ropes, walking, gorge walking or Climbing? These are only a few of many activities you get to take part in. All you need is a sense of adventure and a willingness to give it a go!

Shooting

After intense training from qualified staff. You will get to fire rifles from our Air Rifle right up to our 7.62 Target rifle at paper targets from 25m up to 1000ft! As well as gaining marksman badges, you can compete with the corps against some of the best shooters in the UK.

Sports

The RAFAC supports all different types of sports. As well as team games like rounders, basketball and Volleyball. you can compete against other members of the corps as well as at national level in a number of sports including: Football, Rugby, Hockey & Athletics

Camps

﻿To fit everything in that we offer. cadets get the oppertunity to go away to many different camps all over the world. From a weekends Fieldcraft training at Warcop to a week camp at RAF Cranwell or even a trip to Canada. ﻿

Qualifications

As well as being able to take part in the following qualifications (All accepted by employers and Universities): - BTEC in Aviation Studies (achieved as a part of Master Air Cadet classification) - BTEC Diploma in Public Services - BTEC Diploma in Music - Institute of Leadership & Management Level 2 Award You are also able to gain qualifications in First Aid, Adventure Training, Flying/Gliding and much much more.

Duke of Edinburgh Award

A huge part of 1407 Squadron is our participation in the DofE scheme. All cadets joining will get the chance to progress from Bronze right through to the Gold Award. Achieve this and you get to go to Buckingham Palace and be presented with your award from a member of the Royal Family.

You will be amazed at what activites that the ATC will provide so why don't you join up today?!
